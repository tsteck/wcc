Katherine Johnson

American physicist, space scientist, and mathematician

In 2015, Johnson was awarded Presidential Medal of Freedom.

Katherine Johnson, an African-American space scientist and mathematician, is a leading figure in American space history and has made enormous contributions to America's aeronautics and space programs by her incorporation of computing tools.

Johnson played a huge role in calculating key trajectories in the Space Race--calculating the trajectory for Alan Shepard, the first American in space, as well as for the 1969 Apollo 11 flight to the moon. Johnson is now retired, and continues to encourage students to pursue careers in science and technology fields.

Johnson's contribution to science is truly remarkable.
